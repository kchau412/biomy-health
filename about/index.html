About Biomy

The resource I wish I'd had.

A solo project from Karan, someone living with Ulcerative Colitis, with 18 years of professional experience across consulting, Amazon, and Ford, and 10 years building consumer products.

Diagnosed at 19. Still figuring it out at 35.

I was 19 when I was first diagnosed with Ulcerative Colitis. A gastroenterologist handed me a leaflet about FODMAP, a word that sounds less like a dietary framework and more like an acronym invented by a committee that had given up on being understood. One of its key offenders, oligosaccharides, sounds less like a carbohydrate and more like a Russian oligarch who owns a superyacht.

The condition went into remission. Life moved on. Then, at 35, it came back and stayed for three and a half years. Long enough to reshape how I thought about food, energy, plans, and the particular exhaustion of managing something nobody around you can see.

I needed clear information without the jargon. Guidance built for the food I actually cook. And answers at 11pm, when the next appointment was six weeks away.

Biomy is that thing.

It turns out it wasn't just me.

Millions of people live with gut conditions. The guidance most of them get assumes a Western plate. White bread, apples, plain chicken. It has nothing to say about garlic-and-onion-based Indian cooking, about kimchi, about jollof rice.

The clinical knowledge exists. It just wasn't built for how most of the world actually eats.

Make living with a gut condition a little easier.

Clear guidance without the jargon. Food advice that knows your cuisine, not just a clinical trial's.

Comfort with chronic. That's the whole idea.

Product person. Not a doctor.

I'm not a doctor, a dietitian, or a clinical researcher. I'm a product person who spent too long confused by medical language and under-served by tools that weren't built for how I actually live.
