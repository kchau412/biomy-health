The food guide

What can I actually eat?

FODMAP, in plain language. No science degree required. A simple flag guide to what's usually fine, what depends, and what tends to cause trouble.

FODMAP is a framework developed by Monash University that identifies the short-chain carbohydrates most likely to trigger gut symptoms.

What even is a FODMAP?

You got told to try low-FODMAP and immediately googled "what is a saccharide." Same. It's just an acronym, six letters, each a type of carb that can stir up trouble.

F
Fermentable
Stuff that ferments in your gut like a tiny brewery you didn't ask for.
O
Oligosaccharides
Garlic, onion, wheat. The social butterflies that cause chaos.
D
Disaccharides
Lactose. Milk's personality.
M
Monosaccharides
Fructose, honey, mango, apple, pear. Too much overwhelms your gut.
A
And
Yes, literally just "and." They named a diet after the word "and."
P
Polyols
Stone fruits, cauliflower, mushrooms, sugar-free gum.
